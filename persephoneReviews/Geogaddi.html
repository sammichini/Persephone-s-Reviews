Contemplating Boards of Canada's Geogaddi


Many have described Geogaddi as one of BOC's darker works. Listening to this mysteriously nostalgic album will certainly send you into deep contemplation.
The themes explored are rich and expand far beyond the "dark", into an appeciation for the astounding mathematical nature of music, the wonder of man landing on the moon,
and the urgency of protecting the natural world of the Earth and universe.

The past inside the present

When the album unveiled in January of 2002, it was mostly unannounced and had limited distribution.
It was released during listening parties held at churches in the following cities:

  1. London
  2. New York City
  3. Tokyo
  4. Edinburgh
  5. Berlin
  6. Paris


The darkness shrouding the album is of course no accident. Various other themes present include:

  • Religions and Religious Symbolism
    • Christianity ✝
    • Paganism ⛦
    • Satanism ⛧
  • National Disasters
  • Cults 😵

On the meaning of the album name, member Mike Sandison said:

"Usually, our titles are self-explanatory, but this record's title is a composite that has more than one meaning.
We have a meaning we understand from it, but it's up to listeners to choose their own meaning.
I suppose the nature thing has an indirect effect on us while we're writing, because we're out here in the country most of the time.
We're both heavily interested in science, too, which crosses over into nature and probably comes through in the music."
